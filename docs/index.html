The Reusable Data Project

Inspired by the efforts of scientists around the world and the game-changing efforts of projects like the Creative Commons, Wikimedia Foundation, and the Free Software movement, we hope to engage the larger community in an open and fruitful discussion on issues concerning the use and reuse of scientific data, including the balance of openness and how to make ends meet in an increasingly competitive environment.

If you would like to join our efforts to highlight the use and reuse of data in the sciences, please feel free to contact us on our tracker or create a pull request against our repository.

What this is

The initial driving concern of this project is the use and reuse of biological and biomedical data. However, this is a general problem in the scientific community and needs to be addressed directly.
For each resource, we attempt to assign one of these values objectively by meeting our criteria for reusable data:

  • gold star ★ ★ ★ ★ 
    The license unambiguously allows the /free/ reuse and redistribution of the data.
  • green ★ ★ ★
    The license unambiguously allows reuse and redistribution of the data under some terms.
  • yellow ★ ★
    The license is ambiguous or has terms that prevents the reuse and redistribution of in some cases.
  • red ★
    The license could not be found (so assumed to be under standard copyright) or the license unambiguously makes clear that reuse and redistribution is not welcome for the general community.

If you see any problems with our determinations or would like to make corrections or clarifications, please open a ticket for us on our issue tracker.

Who we are

We are not lawyers, this is not legal advice: all institutions and groups have their own perspectives and counsel. We are a group of scientists, engineers, and specialists that are concerned about the use and reuse of increasingly interconnected, derived, and reprocessed data.

What we want is to provide a resource looking at some of the possible issues with the reuse of scientific data.

We want to actively engage the community in conversation and make sure that our information about scientific data is up-to-date and correct. If you have any questions or see any problems, please open a ticket on our tracker.
If you see an issue, please open a ticket on our GitHub tracker.

Our criteria »

Our criteria

  1. Clearly stated
    Have a clearly stated license for data use. In a lot of ways, this is the most important and time-saving point. If there is no license, then rights are unclear and one needs to assume the default: all rights reserved.
  2. Allow use and reuse
    Data that cannot be used is not data at all. In modern data-driven science, the ability to synthesize data and make the results available for others to do the same is paramount.
  3. Non-discriminatory
    Ideally, data should be able to be used and reused by anybody for any purpose, academic, commercial, and anything else.
  4. Non-revocable
    Of course somebody might choose to close there data off in the future, but there should be no language that revokes usage rights retroactively for the data before that horizon.
  5. Freely and openly available
    (Gold stars) The data should should be available to all people for any purpose. An example of this would be data explicitly released under the Creative Commons 4.0 Attribution 4.0 International license.
